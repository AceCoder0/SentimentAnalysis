How does China's Free Market Economics Square with its Communist Ideology? China’s sustained double-digit growth is one of the great economic success stories of our time. How did China, with its professed Communist ideology and revolutionary history, accomplish such a feat? How do ordinary Chinese as well as their leaders square free-wheeling, free-market success with the collectivist and egalitarian ideals of the Communist movement? In “The Party”, Richard McGregor draws on nearly a decade of business and political reporting in China to find out how the system accommodates a market economy within the big tent of communist ideals. He explains how the system works. “The Party” is an important text for those who seek to understand modern China.
