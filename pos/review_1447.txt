An interesting description of US relations with China over the first half of the 20th century; particularly illustrating the effects of policies conducted by both Presidents Roosevelt. The author highlights the problems, still being felt, resulting from their lack of knowledge of the area and reliance on a small coterie of friends, rather than the expertise of the State Department or people in the field.The book is well written and documented and more oriented to making its case than to strict objectivity.In these days when foreign affairs have become increasingly complex and politicians can reach senior office having little experience outside their own country the book presents a timely warning of the dangers of their running foreign policy without adequate advice.
